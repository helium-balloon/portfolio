Collaboration Manager

Designing and developing platform that streamlines office hours

Overview

The aim of this project is to facilitate CS0320 collaboration hours. At present, collab hours are a little bit hectic, with the TAs trying to call out names to pair students from their google forms, while also trying to help students with escalated issues. This project can ease this experience for students and TAs, and make collab hours more productive. There is also large variety in how collab hours are run and the partnering process, so this project can also standardize collab section.

What are Collab Hours?

CS0320 is the software engineering course at Brown University. This course doesn't have one-on-one debugging hours with TAs as other courses do; rather, there are structured, TA-mentored collab sections where students get debugging helpâ€”both from the TA and from peers. Other courses have long queues to be able to meet with a TA one-on-one, but through this system students can get help right away. The current system is run by TAs without a program to aid the organization of the hours. Below are some key definitions for this project:

  • Help Requester - a student attending hours to obtain help on a question or bug
  • Debugging Partner - a student providing help to students attending hours
  • Instructor - a TA (Teaching Assistant) or course professor who facilitates hours

Research

We started by conducting user interviews with the current course instructor, TAs for the class, and students, as these would be the users of our program. These interviews helped us to understand what features would be most beneficial to develop as well as some stretch goals that could be implemented later.

Completing user research interviews were incredibly useful to help our team understand the priorities for the stakeholders involved in this situation. We used this information gathered from user interviews to create our project plan detailing our project specifications, scope, and timeline. Over the course of 4 weeks, we planned to:

  • Develop concurrent algorithm that matches debugging partners automatically
  • Create easy to use interfaces for instructors, help requesters, and debugging partners
    • Instructors: Dashboard to view all debugging partners and all help requesters as they enter the queues, as well as all current pairs, to monitor the students that are getting help
    • Help Requesters: Dashboard where user can end their debugging session once their debugging partner has finished helping, so debugging partner is reassigned to other students
    • Debugging Partners: Dashboard with easy access to the name of my help requester, the debugging recipe, and a way to escalate a bug to a teaching assistant
  • Download records of all debugging partners to keep track of attendance
  • Flag absent debugging partners
  • Implement timer to let students know how long they are paired so after 15 minutes, the pair can be escalated to the TA level
  • Notify TA when debugging partner needs to escalate a bug for additional help

Solution

We were able to complete all of the features we set out to complete as seen below in the images of our website:

  • Dashboards - Instructor, help requester, debug partner
  • Attendance - Automatically collected from login, download
  • Matching - Based on position in queue
  • Debug Form - Similar to Google form, saves to CSV on backend
  • Escalation - Available after 15 min, displays on instructor dashboard
  • Removal - Remove debug partner or rematch/flag if partnered
